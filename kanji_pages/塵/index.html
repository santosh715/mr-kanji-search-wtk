Koohii stories:

1) [cameron_en] 1-12-2008(40): A deer stomps on the soil and dust rises from the ground. (big ups mantixen).

2) [Megaqwerty] 18-7-2007(12): On the ground, we found the whole body of a deer covered in dust.

3) [Meconium] 29-7-2009(11): This is actually the kanji for garbage, which for some reason is often written in katakana (ゴミ). If you find the whole body of a dead deer outside your apartment, with muddy soil clinging to its fur and its eyeballs covered in dust, make sure you put it in the municipally-supplied printed bag marked 燃やせるゴミ (incinerable waste).

4) [mantixen] 16-2-2007(5): A deer stomps on the soil and is covered in dust.

5) [thecite] 27-4-2010(4): This kanji has two meanings: ゴミ - Trash and チリ - Dust. Both are fairly common.
